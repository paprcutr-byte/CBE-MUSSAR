Savlanut — Patience

CBE Mussar Group · 14 Daily Torah & Wisdom Prompts · Two-Week Practice · May 29 – June 11, 2026

🕯 Two-Week Mantra — Write on an index card and keep it with you
"This too shall pass, and I have the strength to bear the burden until it does."
Also: Erech apayim — "long nose" — draw out the breath and pause before reacting. Every person has their hour, and everything has its place.
Week 1 · May 29 – June 4
1May 29
Where in your life are you carrying the heaviest load of impatience right now?
"The patient in spirit is better than the proud in spirit."
— Kohelet 7:8
2May 30
Think of a moment you lashed out in impatience. What was really going on beneath the surface — whose need was driving it?
"Whoever is slow to anger has great understanding, but one who has a hasty temper exalts folly."
— Proverbs 14:29
3May 31
How does savlanut differ from passive resignation? What does it mean to carry a burden with dignity rather than to simply give up?
"Be still before the Lord and wait patiently for Him."
— Psalm 37:7
4Jun 1
The Mishnah says: "Who is mighty? One who conquers their own impulse." Where did you conquer your impulse toward impatience today?
"Who is mighty? One who subdues their evil inclination."
— Pirkei Avot 4:1
5Jun 2
When is your patience tested most by other people? Can you locate that impatience as a feeling that "this is about me"?
"A patient man calms strife."
— Proverbs 15:18
6Jun 3
Reflect on a time when patience opened a door that anger would have slammed shut. What did that patience cost you? What did it give you?
"Better the end of a thing than its beginning; better patience than pride."
— Kohelet 7:8
7Jun 4
End of Week 1: How has your "patience muscle" changed? Where did you notice improvement — even briefly?
"The one slow to anger is better than the mighty; one who rules their spirit, than the captor of a city."
— Proverbs 16:32
Week 2 · June 5 – June 11
8Jun 5
What recurring situation is still testing your patience? What happens if you stop trying to control it?
"Do not be quick in your spirit to become angry, for anger rests in the breast of fools."
— Kohelet 7:9
9Jun 6
Where did you bear discomfort today without complaint? Bear one another's burdens — the root of savlanut is to carry. What are you carrying for someone else today?
"Bear one another's burdens" — echoing Torah's neighbor-love.
— Leviticus 19:18
10Jun 7
When delay appears, what story do you tell yourself? Try rewriting that story with humility and spaciousness.
"Wait for the Lord; be strong, and let your heart be steadfast; wait for the Lord."
— Psalm 27:14
11Jun 8
Notice the physical sensations of impatience today. Where did you feel them in your body, and what happened when you breathed through them?
"From my narrow place I called to God; God answered me with expansiveness."
— Psalm 118:5
12Jun 9
Who in your life most needs your patience right now? What would it look like to give them room without resentment?
"Love your neighbor as yourself."
— Leviticus 19:18
13Jun 10
What burden feels heavy today? Can you carry it consciously rather than letting it carry you?
"Cast your burden upon the Lord, and He will sustain you; He will never let the righteous be shaken."
— Psalm 55:23
14Jun 11
End of Week 2 — Class Day: What have you learned about savlanut? What one small practice do you want to carry into the next middah?
"Teach us to number our days, that we may gain a heart of wisdom."
— Psalm 90:12
